Scoutfly

Scoutfly is a group-developed tourism app mockup designed to promote and support the discovery of local events and markets through a map-based interface. This project was created by a team of four junior interaction designers over an eight-week period.

Within the team, I focused on wireframing and prototyping as well as collabarting closely with team mates to create a cohesive brand with an unique set of icons. Together we created an app experience that feels intuitive, visually engaging, and uniquely our own for users to enjoy and explore.

Key features of the app include a dynamic map for exploring local events, a personalized algorithm, and a strong filtration feature for a seamless searching process.

Problem:

This project was part of an Univeristy-level assignment to create an interface based on a specfic auidence and issue. My group focused on the issue of, local and niche event information is often spread across multiple platforms, making discovery difficult for users. Meanwhile, organizers struggle to promote their events affordably and effectively, creating a disconnect between events and potential attendees.

Solution:

Scoutfly is an event discovery and promotion app that allows users to explore nearby events through a map-based interface and curated listings, while giving organizers a simple way to promote and track their events in one platform.

Research:

While researching for similar apps already produced that would act as a competitor app, we produced many findings that would benefit our own design process. These platforms highlighted the need for a solution that combined trustful discovery, clear & visual browsing, and location-based navigation.

One competitor TripAdvisor, is a travel app that offers strong searching and browsing features but is found to be unreliable by users. Scoutfly would combat this by requiring user verification for business accounts. Another app for travellers, Fleeps, is great for curating personal experiences but lacks any map functionality. Therefore, we surmised a reliable app with strong filtration and map functions were ideal for users.

Design Process:

Scoutfly’s design process was centred on building a navigation-focused platform that felt clear, easy to use, and welcoming for all users.

Wireframes:

The final prototype builds on the initial wireframes with thoughtful refinements. We created a cohesive, approachable colour scheme and designed intuitive, familiar navigation. The wireframes above highlight the sections I collaborated on, showing early layout decisions and feature development.

Insight Tools:

The initial insight tools for Scoutfly included two distinct personas representing our primary user groups. Jerry reflects the personal user, while Stormi represents the business perspective. From there, we mapped their user journeys to better understand how each would interact with the app.
